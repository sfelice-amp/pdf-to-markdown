ICbS PFS-B Review - Chapter Findings This section provides a narrative summary for each ICbS chapter, reflecting the updated data room contents. 2.1 Chapter 1: Project Management Chapter 1 documentation is now substantially improved. The original 16 construction-focused PDFs are complemented by significant new additions: a Define Study Execution Plan, Define Phase Estimate Plan, area-specific status reports, a Program Management Plan, PEP-10 Construction Management Plan, area-specific Construction Execution Plans (mine, MTS, Teesside, port), Scope Books for each WBS section (1000-5000), a Post-VIP Engineering Report, and seven Projecton-a-Page executive summaries. The Basis Documents folder provides the Basis of Estimate. Remaining gaps are limited to a consolidated capital estimate report, formal OPEX estimate, and commissioning plan. 2.2 Chapter 2-3: Techno-Economic and Financial Evaluation Chapters 2-3 remain the weakest area, though the addition of the FID 2028 Base Case P50 model in the RFIs folder and the Business Case and Modelling Overview presentation provide some coverage. The OPEX revision spreadsheet supports operating cost assessment. However, no formal strategic fit assessment, alternatives analysis, benchmarking study, probabilistic analysis, or standalone financial evaluation report is provided. 2.3 Chapter 6: Processing Chapter 6 is now substantially strengthened. In addition to the original mass and energy balance, energy consumption, R&D strategy, and pilot trial documents, the data room now includes 30 PFDs, 3 BFDs, 60+ P&IDs for processing areas, Basis of Design documents for Teesside (area 3000) and port (area 4000), Design Criteria for process areas, Process Control Philosophy documents, and extensive Trade-Off Studies including hydrogen conversion feasibility. MHF 2.0 design updates with GAs and equipment lists are in the RFIs folder.

8: Energy Chapter 8 folder remains empty but energy-related documentation now exists across other folders. The total energy consumption calculation and construction power demand calculation remain in Ch 6. VIP Technical Notes address substation redundancy optimization (VIP-22, USD 6.8M savings). Trade-Off Studies cover power system design and electrical network configuration. Basis of Design for HV Power Infrastructure (0000-WOB-EL-BD-00001) provides comprehensive energy supply design basis. However, dedicated GHG targets and carbon footprint assessment remain absent. 2.5 Chapter 9: Geotechnical Chapter 9 remains well documented with eight PDFs plus a third-party review presentation. No changes from prior assessment. Strong coverage of geotechnical program, data acquisition, modelling, and site infrastructure across all project sites. 2.6 Chapter 13: Engineering Chapter 13 is now comprehensively documented. The original 6 PDFs are augmented by 12 Basis of Design documents covering all major disciplines and areas, 7 Design Criteria documents, 105 technical drawings (BFDs, PFDs, P&IDs, GAs), 6 Functional Requirements specifications, and 3 Process Control Philosophy documents. The SEAL Plan, dynamic simulation models, and equipment lists from the original data room remain. Engineering management, design basis, standards, infrastructure requirements, and technical drawings are now thoroughly addressed. 2.7 Chapter 15: Environmental Chapter 15 remains the most comprehensively documented discipline with 31 files. The addition of a Permitting & Environment RASCI matrix provides organizational clarity. All prior coverage remains: ESHIRA baseline risk assessment, CEMF, DEMP, biodiversity strategies and site-specific plans, mine closure documentation, chemical and hydrocarbon management, and air quality. The Management Plan Documents folder adds an additional copy of the DEMP and a Water Management Plan. 2.8 Chapter 16: Permitting Chapter 16 coverage remains strong. Permitting Strategy, Constraints and Controls, Permitting Procedure, Resource Plan, compliance guides, and permits register provide comprehensive coverage. No material changes from prior assessment. 2.9 Chapter 19: Marketing Chapter 19 remains empty. No marketing, supply/demand, product strategy, or logistics documentation has been added. This remains a critical gap for PFS-B stage. 2.10 Chapter 23: Commercial Chapter 23 retains its six PDFs covering contracting strategy and procurement plans. The addition of EPCM Work Packages (4 documents defining engineering and construction scope packages) strengthens commercial documentation. The Inclusive Procurement Strategy addresses local procurement. However, contract T&Cs, commercial risk assessment, and insurance documentation remain absent. 2.11 Chapter 25: Risk Management Chapter 25 is now substantially improved with three new documents: H1 and H2 2025 Executive Risk Reports providing senior-level risk oversight, and the Crop Nutrients Integrated Risk Management Plan 2026 which establishes the formal risk management framework with seven key objectives and quarterly milestones. Combined with existing risk registers, WRAC documentation, and workshop outputs, coverage is now strong across most criteria.
